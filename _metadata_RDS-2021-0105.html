Description:
Abstract:
In 1949, a loblolly pine growing space study was established on the Hitchiti Experimental Forest in Georgia as a planned part of a regional growing space study. It is unknown if the rest of the locations were ever established. The study on the Hitchiti was established in even-aged loblolly pine stands of many age classes and densities. This study involved measurements for 72 plots taken roughly every five years by the original research organization through 1983 and then by the current author's unit in 1993 and 2002. Data include plot level information such as location, soil type, stand density as well and basal area both before and after thinning. Measurements throughout the duration of the study have varied across the years, but include tree level information such as species, location, diameter at breast height, tree height, height to base of live crown, crown class, and height of the tree to a 4- or 8-inch top. However, in the early years only expected crop trees were assigned numbers so individual tree growth can only be charted by matching trees based on tally order and diameter.
Purpose:
The study was established to study the relationship between growing space and yield as a function of three variables: age, site, and growing space.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
Up until 1983 all of the individual trees were not tagged, only crop trees. Trees from measurement to measurement were matched based on diameter and azimuth and there could be errors so these data are best used on a plot basis rather than for individual trees. However, the tree matches are generally pretty good and might be useable.

There were also a number of trees whose diameters and heights decreased from measurement to measurement. Some of these reductions can be attributed to the poor growth of the trees and things like broken tops, but not all. These differences are probably the result of different crews measuring the trees and not getting fully accurate measurements.
Logical_Consistency_Report:
The data were checked to confirm that trees did not get smaller and that height to base of live crown was shorter than total height. Also checked was that the height to a 4-inch top was greater than the height to an 8-inch top and that both were shorter than the total height. Duplicate tree numbers were also checked. Additionally, they plots were checked for an increase in number of trees through time, but that check was not helpful since many plots had ingrowth so increases were to be expected. These checks revealed that many trees did get smaller through time either due to actual growth conditions, misidentification of trees, or mismeasurement. We do not think that the number of these problems renders these data without value.

Numbers were not changed unless evidence could be found from the original data sheets to justify the change. If a number was definitely wrong, it was deleted rather than changed to an assumed value. However, this option was used in only a few rare cases.
Completeness_Report:
None of the data that were collected were excluded except for crown width which was only collected in 1963 using unknown methodology. There are several gaps in the time for expected remeasurements. In 1975 only diameters were collected. Several plots have been lost through time due to insects and other factors. Missing data are denoted as blank values. Not every variable was measured at all time periods.

PDF files included in this package are the only format available at this time, as we do not have access to the original documents.

Methodology:
Methodology_Type: Field
Methodology_Description:
PLOT ESTABLISHMENT

The study area selected within the Hitchiti Experimental Forest is in unmanaged, old-field stands. Seventy-two plots were established on the various areas designated for this study from 1948-1949. Grid lines were run from one end of the area to the other at intervals of four chains, and plots were selected along these lines. Permanent sample plots were selected at the time with requirements in mind such as: pure loblolly pine stands; even-aged, 20 years of age or more; homogeneous stands (uniform spacing of trees); site index uniform over the plot; little evidence of disease or insect infestation; and having an area large enough for the plot and isolation strip surrounding the plot (Cooper 1949, Wenger 1954).


THINNING

In the initial thinning, residual densities were prescribed purposely to sample the range of available sites and stand ages. Marking was a trial and error approach to achieve these assigned densities, but was essentially thinning from below. Hardwoods 4.6 inches in diameter and above were poisoned.

Five years after the initial thinning, plots were again thinned to achieve the desired stand density index.

There have been other thinnings throughout the study under the jurisdiction of the original researchers prior to 1993 with no other details available other than the indication of cut trees in the data. In the last two measurements (1993 and 2002) there was no thinning.


MEASUREMENTS

In 1949, 1953/1954, and 1958 hardwoods, cedar trees and pines were the only trees recorded and counts were made by diameter at breast height (dbh) class, so each tree does not have its own record but instead are counts within plot and dbh class. In 1949 the only other additional information recorded was merchantable height to the nearest 1/2 log for trees 9.6 inches dbh or larger and stand age. In 1953/1954 they added tree height measurements as well as crown class. In 1958 they added measurements of height to base of live crown.

In 1964 they stopped recording eastern red cedar but started recording individual tree measurements for shortleaf pine and loblolly pine, and other new species (e.g., sweetgum). They also started measuring the height of the tree to a 4-inch top. Measurements then continued roughly every 5 years: 1969, 1975, and 1983. Measurements were discontinued until 1993 when they went back to the plots and recorded species, dbh, tree height, height to base of live crown, and crown class for each tree in the plot as well as stand age. In 2002 species, dbh, tree height, height to base of live crown and stand age were recorded. In 1993, not all hardwoods were recorded based on the justification that they would be killed, but they were not.

Trees that were downed or dead were recorded and are provided in the data file, but dbh, height, etc. were not measured.

Plot information such as location, soil type, stand density as well and basal area both before and after thinning were also recorded.


For more details see Cooper (1949), Brender (1954) and Wenger (1954) (all included in the data publication download).

Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
Below you will find a list and description of the files included in this data publication.

DATA FILES (2)

1. \Data\hitchiti.csv: Comma-delimited ASCII text file containing plot level information such as soil type, stand density as well and basal area both before and after thinning, as well as tree measurements such as whether a tree was marked for cutting, diameter at breast height, tree height, height to base of live crown, crown class, etc.

Variables included in this file are defined below.

- Plot-level Information -

Plotnum: The identifying number of the plot.

Latitude: Latitude of the plot in decimal degrees.

Longitude: Longitude of the plot in decimal degrees. The negative sign means west which applies to all plots

Source: Some of the plots were checked in the field using a consumer grade GPS unit and these are labelled gps. The remaining plots were no longer available at the latest remeasurement or were omitted due to the crew that measured them and these locations come from the maps in the establishment report. They are labelled map.

Elevation: Elevation above sea level in feet. This was obtained by interpreting between 10-foot contour intervals from a USGS downloaded shapefile.

Soil: There was soils information in the report: Brender, Ernst V. Establishment report for growing space study on Hitchiti Experimental Forest. Supplement No. 1 to plan of Sept. 1949. December 1949. (The information provided below was obtained from notes taken by Leduc after going through Hitchiti Experimental Forest files many years ago.) The soil series was codified for simplicity.
Cy: Cecil Sandy loam
Dh: Davidson clay loam
Hy: Helena fine sandy loam
Lf: Lloyd clay loam
Lo : Lloyd sandy loam
Ma: Madison fine sandy loam
Md: Madison clay loam
Me: Mecklenburg fine sandy loam
Se: Seneca sandy loam
Va: Vance fine sandy loam
VaD: Vance fine sandy loam, deep phase
VaR: Vance fine sandy loam, red subsoil phase
VaSR: Vance fine sandy loam, severely eroded red subsoil phase
We: Wehadkee loam
WI: Wilkes sandy loam
Plot 40 is a borderline soil which favors Lloyd clay loam but distinct contour furrows favor moisture retention. Plot 3 is a borderline soil complex which is not well identified. Plot 47 has no apparent difference in soil type. Plot 15 has no apparent difference in soil type. Plot 13 was not described in my notes from the above report so I went to the current soil map to identify it.

SI: Site index at base age 50 in feet. This was obtained by taking increment cores at 4.5 feet of several dominant and codominant trees on each plot. Age was adjusted based on previously clearcut stands based on site index. If the site index was less than 50, 6 years were added; between 50 and 60, 5 years were added; between 61 and 70, 4 years were added; and at 71 or more, 3 years were added. Total height was measured with an Abney level and then average total age and height was used to find a site index value from the site curves for loblolly pine growing in even-aged, fully-stocked stands as presented in McKinney and Chaiken (1939).

SDI_before: Stand density index in 1949 before any thinning of cutting or thinning was done.

SDI_after: Stand density index in 1949 after planned cutting or thinning was completed. (In some cases there was no planned cutting or thinning so we just repeated the SDI_before number here.)

BA_before: Basal area in square feet per acre before any thinning action. Basal area was only reported for those plots that were thinned. It is missing otherwise.

BA_after: Basal area in square feet per acre after any planned thinning action. Basal area was only reported for those plots that were thinned. It is missing otherwise.

Born: The year that the stand was started. This was determined by the stand age in 1949 minus 1949. It is useful for determining age at any given year.


- Tree Variables -

Treeno: The tree ID number. In 1949 the trees were not numbered so I created a number above 700 that will not match up to future records. In other years I’ve numbered hardwoods which were not originally numbered with numbers above 800. When the plots were remeasured in 1993 there was not perfect correspondence between the tree locations used in 1983 and the 1993 tree locations, although a reasonable attempt was made to match trees. If you are charting the growth of a single tree you may not want to use the growth interval between 1983 and 1993. I will show both the old azimuth and the new azimuth so that you can better judge.

Old_Azimuth: Azimuth in degrees from plot center to center or tree as recorded prior to 1993. When the plots were remeasured in 1993, the trees did not completely align with the measurements taken earlier, but in the event that there is a question of tree id this old information might help.

Azimuth: Azimuth in degrees from plot center to tree as recorded 1993 and after.

Distance: Distance in feet from plot center to tree. This measurement was only started in 1993.


- Measurement Variables -

Count: In the early measurements (1949, 1954, 1958) Hardwoods, cedar trees, and pulpwood-sized pines (< 10-inch dbh) were just counted by diameter at breast height (dbh) class so each tree does not have its own record. They are grouped with a count per plot.

Year: The year of measurement. This can be used with plot variable born to determine age of plot.

0 = not cut
